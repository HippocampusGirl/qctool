HPTEST fits the following model: $$\text{log-odds}( Y = 1 ) = X^t \cdot \beta$$ where $Y$ is the outcome genotype, $X$ is a function of the predictor genotype and covariates, $$X = \left( \begin{matrix} 1 \\ F(\text{predictor genotype}) \\ Z \end{matrix} \right)$$ and $\beta$ is a vector of regression coefficients to be maximised over. The full likelihood is obtained as the product of the above over all samples in the data. HPTEST currently assumes that both outcome and predictor variants are biallelic (or at least that they have been split into biallelic records before running through HPTEST). Thus $Y=1$ denotes a call of the second allele. *Handling ploidy*. If the outcome has ploidy larger than 1, then its alleles are regarded as forming independent draws from the Bernoulli distribution modelled above. *Handling mode of inheritance*. The predictor genotype is currently assumed to be diploid. To handle different possible modes of inheritance, let $g \in \{0,1,2\}$ denote the count of the second allele in the predictor genotype; then the function $F$ is defined as follows: Mode of inheritance | value of F | Specified by ------------------- | ---------- | -------------------- additive | $F(g) = g$ | ``-model add`` dominant | $F(g) = 1$ if $g > 0$, or $0$ otherwise | ``-model dom`` recessive | $F(g) = 1$ if $g = 2$, or $0$ otherwise | ``-model rec`` heterozygote | $F(g) = 1$ if $g = 1$, or $0$ otherwise | ``-model het`` general | $F(g) = (g,1)$ if $g = 1$, or $(g,0)$ otherwise | ``-model add+het``
