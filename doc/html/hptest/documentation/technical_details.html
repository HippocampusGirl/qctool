HPTEST currently assumes that both outcome and predictor variants are biallelic (or at least that they have been split into biallelic records before running through HPTEST). The outcome and predictor genotypes are measured with respect to those alleles, i.e. the effect is always interpreted as effect of predictor allele 2 on outcome allele 2. If the outcome has ploidy larger than 1, then its alleles are regarded as forming independent draws from the Bernoulli distribution modelled above.
